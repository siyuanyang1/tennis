Introduction & History

Gender inequality in tennis has been a longstanding issue particularly for women players who have faced disparities in prize money, media coverage, and opportunities compared to their male counterparts.

Example: Billie Jean King was awarded $600 while her male counterpart Ilie Nastase was awarded $3500 at the Italian Open.

Timeline:

  • 1970: Billie Jean King was awarded $10,000 while Nastase was awarded $25,000 at the US Open.
  • 1973: Billie Jean King threatened to boycott the US Open due to unequal pay; US Open becomes the first Grand Slam to offer equal prize money.

Gender Pay Gap

Despite significant progress, challenges persist in prize money equality. For instance, Iga Swiatek earned less than her male counterparts in 2023 despite a dominant season.

Examples:

  • In 2023, Iga Swiatek had one of the most dominant seasons of any tennis player, claiming two of the four Grand Slams and winning 37 straight matches. However, by the end of the calendar year, Novak Djokovic and Carlos Alcaraz had both made more money from on-court performance despite each winning only one major and the former playing in six fewer tournaments than Swiatek.
  • The prize money for the Cincinnati Open, a Masters 1000 ATP and WTA event, was $6.6 million for the men but only $2.79 million for women.

Personal Experience and Advice

Michelle Yang shares her experiences as a competitive junior tennis player, highlighting issues like unequal training opportunities and disrespectful behavior towards female players.

Examples:

  • Training facilities and coaches focus more on developing boys over girls.
  • Boys receive more personal attention and private instruction.
  • During co-ed group practices, boys receive more attention from all staff.
  • Verbal insults towards the WTA with the goal of degrading their players.

Advice:
